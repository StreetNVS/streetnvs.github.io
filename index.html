StreetNVS: Multi-Source Geometry Guidance
for Street-View Novel View Generation

Reconstruction-based methods interpolate well between observed views but fail when target poses depart sharply from the vehicle trajectory; purely generative methods produce plausible appearance without geometric consistency. We bridge the two by using LiDAR-grounded 3D anchors and relative camera poses to condition a controllable video generation backbone, preserving identity and structure under extreme extrapolation.

§1 · Unseen novel view synthesis

Synthesizing trajectories far from the vehicle path

For each scene we render four camera trajectories that depart from the original ego-trajectory: Barron (close to source), Elevate (BEV-like), Lane Shift (sideways translation), and Rotate (yaw rotation). Drag the slider on any clip to wipe between the synthesized RGB output (left) and its underlying LiDAR conditioning (right).

§2 · Comparison with baselines

Side-by-side against state-of-the-art

Each block compares ground-truth and our LiDAR conditioning (top row) with three camera-only baselines—FreeVS, Gen3C, VACE—and three LiDAR-aware methods: StreetCrafter, StreetCrafter* (an in-domain re-implementation), and Ours. The first two scenes are the anchors used in the paper; the last two are extrapolation cases at sparsity ratio 0.1 where StreetNVS shows the largest PSNR gain over StreetCrafter*.

§3 · Ablation study

Each conditioning signal carries weight

Removing the LiDAR scaffold, the relative-camera-pose stream, or the reference view each degrades a different aspect of consistency. Top row pairs the ground truth with our full model; the bottom row shows the three ablations stripped of one signal at a time.

§4 · Demo on variable LiDAR sparsity

How much LiDAR is enough?

Pick a scene and sweep the density slider between 0.1% and 100% of the original LiDAR points. The middle panel shows what the model actually receives as conditioning; the right panel is StreetNVS's output. Even at 0.1% density, structure and identity hold up, while the appearance gradually sharpens as more anchors come back.
